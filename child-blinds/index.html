Child Blinds FAQs

Common questions about safety, fitting, and choosing the right blind for your child's room.

Are all of your child blinds cordless?
Every blind we fit in a child's bedroom or nursery uses a cordless wand, spring-loaded chain tensioner, or fully enclosed mechanism as standard, in line with UK child safety regulations for corded blinds. If you'd prefer a specific safety mechanism, just let us know during your home consultation.
Do child-safe blinds cost more than standard blinds?
Cordless wand controls and chain tensioners are fitted as standard on every child blind at no extra charge. Pricing depends mainly on fabric, size, and blind type rather than the safety mechanism itself.
What's the difference between blackout and day & night blinds for a nursery?
Blackout blinds use a fully light-blocking fabric, ideal for helping babies and toddlers nap during the day and sleep through lighter summer evenings. Day & night blinds use alternating sheer and solid stripes so you can soften daylight for playtime, then slide to full privacy at bedtime, all from one blind.
Can I get child-safe blinds fitted without drilling into the window frame?
Yes. Our no-drill, Perfect Fit style child blinds clip directly onto the window frame using spring-loaded brackets, so there's no drilling into the frame or surrounding wall. This is a popular choice for rented homes and new nurseries.
I already have blinds in my child's room. Can you make them safer?
Often, yes. We stock child safety clips, cord cleats, and chain tensioners, and can retrofit these to most existing blinds during a home visit.
