Made to Measure, Room by Room

Living rooms and bedrooms tend to want different things from a curtain, and we tailor the fabric and lining to suit. In a living room, we'll usually recommend a fuller pleat and a mid-weight fabric that drapes well and frames the window during the day. In a bedroom, blackout or thermal linings do the heavy lifting, while a softer voile underneath keeps things bright and airy when the main curtains are open.

Curtains FAQs

Common questions about measuring, fitting, and choosing the right curtains for your home.

How do I know what drop and width to order?
You don't need to measure a thing yourself. As part of your free home consultation, we measure the exact width and drop for each window, taking into account your chosen pole or track and how full you'd like the pleats to hang.
Can I have curtains and blinds fitted together on the same window?
Yes, this is one of our most popular combinations. A voile or blackout roller blind sits neatly behind the window recess for daily light control, while curtains add texture, warmth, and a finished look to the room.
Do thermal curtains really reduce energy bills?
A thermal lining adds a genuine layer of insulation against the glass, reducing heat loss through the window in winter and keeping rooms cooler in direct summer sun, which can take a noticeable edge off heating costs in older or single-glazed homes.
What lining options are available for blackout curtains?
We offer standard blackout lining for full light block, as well as thermal-blackout combination linings that add insulation alongside light control. Both are available across our eyelet and pencil pleat headings.
Can you make curtains to match blinds you've already fitted in my home?
Yes. During your consultation we can coordinate fabric tones and textures with any existing Blissful Blinds products in your home, so new curtains complement rather than clash with your current blinds or shutters.
